Introduction

Well, if you look at the graph of my academic progress, you will notice that I started at the top then I touched rock bottom and I came back on top again. So I truly believe, “The only constant in life is change."

Since secondary school, I have wanted to do something related to computers. Electronics gadgets always amazed me in my childhood. I repaired a relative's mobile phone when I was in the 9th standard and I used to bring that mobile every day to school just to give a call to the teacher's mobile number while he was teaching in the class. As soon as he picks up I would hang up and the teacher would keep saying hello, hello, hello. The whole class used to laugh and the teacher would scratch his head with confusion. Fun part is teachers didn't catch me for the next 2 years.
I really don't know, if is it relevant to mention this story here but my journey toward computers / IT started from this scenario.

I went off track in 11th ― 12th. I got distracted. All the things one can possibly think an ideal college student won't do. I have been there, done that. When I appeared for the 12th board exam, believe me, I didn't even know the color of a single textbook, let alone touch it. We called it a miracle that I passed the exam. I wasn't interested in Engineering, I wanted to pursue bachelors in computer science. So I came to Pune to get my life back on the right track and never looked back.

“Tomorrow is my exam but I don’t care because a single sheet of paper can’t decide my future.”

If Thomas Edison was from India, he would have never said this. Because in India, a piece of paper is everything.


A piece of paper (BIRTH CERTIFICATE) decides if you exist. A piece of paper (MARKSHEET) decides if you are well educated.
A piece of paper (EXPERIENCE LETTER) decides if you are experienced. A piece of paper (AADHAR CARD) decides if you are a civilian.
A piece of paper (PASSPORT) decides if you can travel around the world. A piece of paper (SALARY SLIP) decides if you are a good match/groom.
A piece of paper (NEWSPAPER) decides if everything around you is fine. A piece of paper (MONEY) decides your reputation in society.
A piece of paper (TICKET) decides if you are authorized to aboard public transport. A piece of paper (PROPERTY DOCUMENT) decides if you are the owner.
A piece of paper (FIR) decides the results of your unlawful activities. A piece of paper (CHESSBOARD) decides if you are brilliant.
A piece of paper (ITR) decides your contribution toward nation building. A piece of paper (DEATH CERTIFICATE) decides if you are dead.
It's because of a piece of paper (RESUME) you are reading this and the same piece of paper decides if I am a good match for an organization.
